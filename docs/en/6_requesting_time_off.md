# Requesting Time Off & Sick Leaves (Daily English Edition)

In a professional IT environment, requesting time off (PTO), calling in sick, or stepping away for a personal errand requires clear, polite, and timely communication. You do not need to share intimate personal details. Keep your messages short, professional, and outcome-focused.

---

## 1. Academic vs. Daily Leave English

Avoid overly formal, stiff, and academic expressions. Modern global software companies prefer clear, friendly, and direct conversational phrases.

| ❌ Academic / Too Formal (Avoid) | 🟢 Daily & Conversational (Use instead) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "I hereby request authorization to execute a period of personal leave." | "I'd like to take a few days off next week." |
| "I am experiencing health issues and will not present myself for duty." | "I'm feeling under the weather today, so I'll be taking a sick day." |
| "I am departing for a medical consultation." | "I need to step away for a doctor's appointment." |
| "Please ensure my duties are covered during my absence." | "John will cover for me while I'm away." |
| "I request permission to absent myself due to personal exigencies." | "I need to take a personal day for some private matters." |
| "Due to unforeseen circumstances, I cannot perform my tasks." | "Something unexpected came up, so I won't be able to log in today." |
| "I shall resume my professional activities on..." | "I'll be back online on..." |
| "I regret to inform you that I must request retroactive approval for my absence." | "Sorry I couldn't notify you earlier, I had to take an emergency day off yesterday." |
| "I am desirous of taking a sabbatical period." | "I'd like to request an unpaid leave of absence." |
| "Please deduct this from my accrued leave balance." | "Please use my PTO balance for this." |
| "I wish to inform you of my unavailability on the morrow." | "Just wanted to let you know I'll be out of the office tomorrow." |
| "My physical state precludes me from engaging in software development." | "I'm not feeling well enough to work today." |

## 2. Key Terms & Abbreviations to Know

Master these 17 terms to navigate leave requests, HR systems, and calendar updates effortlessly:

1. **PTO (Paid Time Off):** Vacation days or personal leave where you are still paid.
   * *"I'm taking 3 days of PTO next week."*
2. **OOO (Out of Office):** Being away from work and unavailable.
   * *"I will be OOO next Monday."*
3. **Under the weather:** A professional idiom meaning "feeling sick."
   * *"I woke up feeling under the weather."*
4. **Cover for [someone]:** To handle a colleague's urgent tasks while they are away.
   * *"Can you cover for me during the standup?"*
5. **Hand over / Handover:** Transferring tasks and context to a backup person.
   * *"I will complete the handover to Huy before I log off."*
6. **Annual Leave:** Vacation days allowed by your contract.
   * *"I'm planning to use my annual leave in June."*
7. **Family Emergency:** A sudden, urgent situation involving a family member.
   * *"I have a family emergency and need to log off immediately."*
8. **Maternity/Paternity Leave:** Leave for new mothers/fathers.
   * *"He is currently on paternity leave."*
9. **Bereavement Leave:** Leave granted due to the death of a close relative.
   * *"She requested bereavement leave to attend the funeral."*
10. **Unpaid Leave / Leave of Absence:** Time off work without pay.
    * *"I need to request two weeks of unpaid leave."*
11. **Comp Off (Compensatory Off):** Rest day given in exchange for working overtime/weekends.
    * *"I'm taking a comp off today since I worked on Saturday."*
12. **Half-day:** Taking only the morning or afternoon off.
    * *"I'd like to request a half-day off tomorrow afternoon."*
13. **Leave Balance:** The number of remaining paid vacation days.
    * *"My leave balance is down to 5 days."*
14. **Public Holiday:** Official national holidays.
    * *"The office will be closed on Friday due to a public holiday."*
15. **Bridging Holiday:** A day off taken to connect a holiday and a weekend.
    * *"I'm taking a bridging holiday on Monday since Tuesday is a national holiday."*
16. **Sabbatical:** A long period of paid or unpaid leave for study, travel, or rest.
    * *"He is taking a three-month sabbatical next year."*
17. **Doctor's Note / Medical Certificate:** Official paper from a doctor verifying you were sick.
    * *"I will send HR the doctor's note for my sick leave."*

## 3. Fill-in-the-blank Email & Slack Templates

### Template A: Planned PTO Request (Email to Manager)
Use this when asking for vacation days in advance.
> **Subject:** PTO Request - **[Your Name]** - **[Start Date]** to **[End Date]**
>
> Hi **[Manager's Name]**,
>
> I'd like to request some time off next month. I am planning to be out of the office from **[Start Date]** to **[End Date]**, returning on **[Return Date]**.
>
> Before I leave, I will make sure all my current tasks are wrapped up. **[Colleague's Name]** has agreed to cover for any urgent issues while I am away.
>
> Please let me know if this timing works for the team.
>
> Thanks,
> **[Your Name]**

### Template B: Sick Day Notification (Slack/Teams Message to Team)
Send this as early as possible on the day you are sick.
> "Hi team, I woke up feeling under the weather today, so I need to take a sick day to rest. I will be offline. For any urgent issues with my project, please reach out to **[Colleague's Name]** who can cover for me. See you tomorrow!"

### Template C: Stepping Away for 1-2 Hours (Slack/Teams Message)
Send this in the team chat before you leave for a doctor's appointment or personal errand.
> "Hey team, I need to step away for a quick doctor's appointment. I will be offline from **[Time, e.g., 2 PM]** to **[Time, e.g., 4 PM]**. I'll have my phone on me for emergencies, and I'll catch up on messages as soon as I get back."

### Template D: Out of Office (OOO) Auto-responder Email
Set this up in Outlook/Gmail before you log off for vacation.
> **Subject:** Out of Office: **[Your Name]**
>
> Thank you for your email. I am currently out of the office on annual leave and will return on **[Return Date]**.
>
> During this time, I will have no access to my email. For urgent technical matters, please contact **[Colleague's Name]** at **[Colleague's Email]**. Otherwise, I will reply to your message upon my return.
>
> Best regards,
> **[Your Name]**

### Template E: Requesting a Half-Day Off (Slack/Teams Message)
> "Hi team, I need to request a half-day off tomorrow afternoon for personal matters. I'll be working in the morning until **[Time, e.g., 12 PM]** and will be offline after that. **[Colleague's Name]** will cover my tasks for the rest of the day."

### Template F: Requesting a Compensatory Off (Comp-off) (Email/Slack to Manager)
> "Hi **[Manager's Name]**, since I worked last Saturday to assist with the production release, I'd like to take a compensatory day off this Friday, **[Date]**. My tasks are on track, and Huy will cover for me if anything urgent arises. Please let me know if that is okay."

### Template G: Urgent Family/Personal Emergency (Slack Message)
> "Hi team, a family emergency has come up unexpectedly, and I need to log off immediately. I won't be able to complete my tasks today. I've asked Huy to monitor my active deployment. I'll update you as soon as I can."

### Template H: Long-Term Sabbatical/Unpaid Leave Request (Email to Manager)
> **Subject:** Leave of Absence Request - **[Your Name]**
>
> Hi **[Manager's Name]**,
>
> I am writing to formally request a temporary leave of absence for personal reasons. I would like to request unpaid leave from **[Start Date]** to **[End Date]**.
>
> I understand this is a significant request, and I want to ensure my absence does not disrupt the team. I am happy to help train a backup developer and document all my workflows before the leave begins.
>
> I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this further during our 1-on-1 this week.
>
> Best regards,
> **[Your Name]**

## 4. Grammar Hacks for Polite Requests

When asking for permission, using softeners and conditionals will make your request much more polite and less demanding.

### 4.1. "I was wondering if I could..." (Past continuous for politeness)
* *"I **was wondering if I could** take next Friday off."*
* *"I **was wondering if it would be possible to** adjust my hours tomorrow."*
* *"I **was wondering if it's okay if I** work from home this week."*

### 4.2. "Would you mind if I..." + Past Simple verb
* *"**Would you mind if I took** a personal day on Monday?"*
* *"**Would you mind if I logged off** early today?"*
* *"**Would you mind if I swapped** my shifts with Huy on Thursday?"*

### 4.3. "Would it be okay if I..."
* *"**Would it be okay if I worked** from home tomorrow morning?"*
* *"**Would it be okay to** reschedule our sync to Thursday?"*
* *"**Would it be okay if I used** my remaining leave balance next week?"*

### 4.4. "I'd appreciate it if I could..."
* *"I **would highly appreciate it if I could** take a day off to attend to a family matter."*
* *"I **would appreciate it if I could** take a bridging holiday on Friday."*

## 5. Handling Urgent & Emergency Situations

Sometimes you need to log off immediately due to unplanned issues. Use these quick Slack templates:

### 5.1. Internet / Power Outage (Mất mạng / Mất điện)
* *"Hey team, my area is currently experiencing a power outage / internet blackout. I will be offline until it's resolved. I'm keeping an eye on my phone for urgent alerts."*
* *"My internet is down at the moment. I've contacted the provider. I should be back online in about an hour."*
* *"I'm having connectivity issues with my local internet provider. I will try to connect via 4G, but please expect some delays in my replies."*
* *"Power is completely out at my place. I'm moving to a coffee shop nearby and should be online again in 30 minutes."*

### 5.2. Family/Personal Emergency (Việc gia đình đột xuất)
* *"Hi team, a family emergency has come up, and I need to log off for the rest of the day. I'll make sure to catch up on my tasks tomorrow."*
* *"Hey [Manager Name], I need to take urgent leave today due to a personal emergency. I've asked [Colleague Name] to monitor staging alerts."*
* *"I have to step away immediately due to a family emergency. I will update you later on my availability for tomorrow."*
* *"Logging off early today due to a sudden personal emergency. I will be offline, but you can call me if there is a production blocker."*

## 6. How to Handle Pushbacks (Sếp yêu cầu làm việc lúc đang nghỉ)

If a manager or client asks you to look at a bug during your vacation, protect your boundaries professionally:

* **If you have absolutely no internet access:**
  * *"I am currently traveling and have no access to my computer or internet. Please reach out to [Colleague Name] as they have full context on this module."*
  * *"I am completely offline during my PTO and do not have my laptop with me. I suggest checking with [Colleague Name] to see if they can help."*
* **If it's an emergency but you can only look at it later:**
  * *"I'm currently away from my desk. I can check this in a couple of hours if it's still unresolved, but otherwise, [Colleague Name] is covering my sprint tasks today."*
  * *"I cannot log in right now. I will have access to my laptop around 8 PM tonight and can take a look then if it's still urgent."*
* **Politely directing to the official backup person:**
  * *"As mentioned in my OOO status, [Colleague Name] is covering for me today. They should be able to assist you with this issue."*
  * *"I am currently on leave, but [Colleague Name] is fully briefed on this release and can help you troubleshoot."*

## 7. Step-by-Step Walkthrough Dialogue (Kịch bản thực tế)

Here is how a conversation usually goes between a Developer and a Manager:

* **Dev:** *"Hi [Manager], I was wondering if it would be okay for me to take three days of PTO next week, from Wednesday to Friday."*
* **Manager:** *"Hi, thanks for asking. How is the progress on the payment dashboard? Who is covering for you?"*
* **Dev:** *"The main dashboard features are already merged. I will finish the remaining unit tests by Monday. Huy has agreed to cover for me for any urgent deployment issues while I am OOO."*
* **Manager:** *"Excellent. The plan looks solid. Have a great time off!"*
* **Dev:** *"Thanks, I appreciate it. I'll make sure to set up my OOO responder and update my Slack status before I log off on Tuesday."*

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

* **Sharing Too Much Personal Information (TMI):**
  * ❌ *“I need to stay home because I have stomach issues and bad diarrhea.”*
  * 🟢 *“I'm feeling under the weather today, so I'll be taking a sick day to rest.”*
* **Not Naming a Backup Contact:**
  * ❌ *“I will be off tomorrow. Bye.”*
  * 🟢 *“I will be OOO tomorrow. For any urgent queries, Huy has agreed to cover for me.”*
* **Requesting Too Late for Planned Leave:**
  * Always request planned vacations at least one sprint in advance so the team can adjust the velocity.
* **Being Vague About Return Dates:**
  * ❌ *“I will take some days off and come back later next week.”*
  * 🟢 *“I will be on leave from Wednesday to Friday, returning to the office next Monday.”*
* **Asking for Permission via Slack at the Last Minute (for planned leave):**
  * ❌ *“Hey, I'm taking tomorrow off. Hope that's okay.”* (Unless it is an emergency, this is unprofessional).
  * 🟢 *“Hi [Manager Name], I'd like to request tomorrow off for a personal errand. All my tasks are currently on track.”*
