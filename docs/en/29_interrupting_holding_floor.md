# Interrupting & Holding the Floor

In fast-paced IT meetings, especially with native speakers, it can be difficult to get your voice heard or to prevent others from cutting you off. You need polite but firm English phrases to interrupt when necessary and to hold your ground when someone tries to take the floor from you.

## 1. Polite Interruption (When You Need to Speak)

If you need to add an important technical point, correct a misunderstanding, or ask a question, do it politely.

- "Sorry to interrupt, but I wanted to add a quick point about the database schema."
- "If I could just jump in here for a second, we actually solved a similar issue on another project by doing A instead of B."
- "Before we move to the next slide, can I ask a quick question about the authentication flow?"
- "Sorry, just a quick clarification on that point..."

## 2. Firm Interruption (When You Must Stop Someone)

If someone is sharing incorrect information or going off-topic, you may need to interrupt more firmly.

- "Sorry, [Name], let me stop you there for a second. That endpoint is actually deprecated; we should use the new `/v2/` API instead."
- "Actually, if I may, that’s not quite correct. The staging database is backed up daily, not weekly."
- "I think we are going slightly off-topic. Let’s bring the focus back to the release plan."

## 3. Holding the Floor (When Someone Interrupts You)

If someone tries to cut you off before you finish your explanation, use these phrases to keep speaking. Keep your tone professional but assertive.

- "Just let me finish this thought, and then I’d be happy to take your questions."
- "If I could just complete this explanation first, it might answer your question."
- "Hold that thought, [Name]. I will cover the database migration details in the next slide."
- "I’m almost done, just give me one more minute."

## 4. Retaking the Floor (When You've Been Cut Off)

If someone successfully interrupted you and finished speaking, here is how you take back control of the conversation.

- "Going back to what I was saying earlier about the performance bottlenecks..."
- "As I was saying before we discussed the styling changes..."
- "To pick up where I left off..."

## 5. Giving the Floor to Others

If you want to invite someone else to speak or pass the microphone back to the presenter.

- "I’ll pass the floor back to [Name] to walk us through the demo."
- "I’d love to hear [Name]’s input on this since she is the lead architect."
- "That’s all from my side. Over to you, [Name]."
