# PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

## Essential English phrases for annual and mid-year performance evaluations

> **Purpose of this document**
>
> - Provide Vietnamese software engineers with ready-to-use phrases for performance review meetings
> - Help you prepare self-assessments, present achievements, and discuss improvement areas
> - Offer templates for receiving feedback, setting goals, and following up professionally
> - Build confidence for review conversations with English-speaking managers

---

# 1. PREPARING FOR YOUR REVIEW

Before walking into a performance review, thorough preparation is essential. Start by gathering data about your contributions at least one week in advance.

## 1.1. Self-Assessment Phrases

Use these phrases in your self-assessment document or review preparation notes:

**Opening your self-assessment:**
- "Over the past review period, I have consistently contributed to [team/project] by..."
- "During this cycle, my primary focus areas were..."
- "I would like to highlight three key areas of contribution this period..."
- "Reflecting on the past six months, I am proud of the progress I have made in..."
- "This review period has been a significant growth opportunity for me, particularly in..."

**Describing your overall contribution:**
- "I took ownership of [project] and successfully delivered it on schedule."
- "I proactively identified and resolved [issue] before it impacted the client."
- "I contributed beyond my core responsibilities by..."
- "I maintained a high standard of code quality, as reflected in..."
- "I adapted quickly to changing priorities when..."

## 1.2. Documenting Achievements with Metrics

Always quantify your work when possible. Numbers speak louder than words in reviews.

**Using metrics effectively:**
- "Reduced API response time by 40%, from 800ms to under 500ms."
- "Delivered 12 feature releases with zero critical bugs in production."
- "Increased test coverage from 45% to 78% across the core module."
- "Resolved 95% of assigned tickets within the agreed SLA timeframe."
- "Mentored 2 junior developers, both of whom successfully completed their probation period."

**When you don't have hard numbers:**
- "Streamlined the deployment process, reducing manual steps significantly."
- "Improved the onboarding documentation, which received positive feedback from new team members."
- "Led knowledge-sharing sessions that improved team velocity on similar tasks."
- "Played a key role in stabilizing the production environment during Q3."
- "Received consistent positive feedback from the client on communication and delivery quality."

# 2. TALKING ABOUT ACHIEVEMENTS

Presenting your work positively is not bragging -- it is an essential professional skill. Use structured formats to make your achievements clear and impactful.

## 2.1. Templates for Presenting Your Work

**The "Delivered-Improved-Achieved" framework:**
- "I **delivered** the payment integration module two weeks ahead of schedule, which allowed the team to start UAT earlier."
- "I **improved** the CI/CD pipeline build time by 35%, reducing the average build from 12 minutes to under 8 minutes."
- "I **achieved** a 99.5% uptime for the reporting service during the holiday peak season."
- "I **led** the migration from REST to GraphQL for 15 endpoints, improving data fetching efficiency."
- "I **implemented** automated regression tests that catch an average of 8 bugs before each release."

**Talking about collaborative achievements:**
- "I collaborated with the design team to refine the user experience, resulting in a 20% increase in user engagement."
- "I worked closely with the client to clarify requirements, reducing scope changes by 30%."
- "I partnered with the QA team to establish a more efficient testing workflow."
- "I supported the backend team by providing clear API documentation, which reduced integration issues."
- "I contributed to architectural decisions that improved system scalability for future growth."

## 2.2. Handling the "Tell Me About Your Biggest Accomplishment" Question

- "My biggest accomplishment this period was leading the [project name] initiative. I coordinated with three teams to deliver a solution that reduced processing time by 60%."
- "I am most proud of stepping up as acting tech lead during [manager's] leave. I kept the team on track and delivered the sprint goals without missing any deadlines."
- "One accomplishment that stands out is resolving the long-standing memory leak issue that had been affecting production for months. It improved system stability significantly."

# 3. DISCUSSING AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT

Every developer has areas to improve. The key is to frame them as growth opportunities rather than weaknesses.

## 3.1. Framing Weaknesses Positively

**Using the "Recognize-Action-Result" pattern:**
- "I recognize that my skills in [area] need improvement. To address this, I have been [action], and I am already seeing progress in [result]."
- "One area I want to develop further is [skill]. I have started [action] and plan to [next step] by [timeframe]."
- "Early in the period, I struggled with [challenge]. I addressed this by [action], and now I feel much more confident."
- "I acknowledge that [weakness] has been a challenge. I have taken concrete steps, including [action], to improve in this area."
- "While I have made progress in [area], I see room for continued growth, and I plan to focus on [specific goal] next period."

**Common improvement areas with sample phrases:**
- **Communication:** "I am working on being more proactive in sharing updates with stakeholders. I have started sending weekly status summaries and scheduling brief check-ins."
- **Time estimation:** "I have been refining my estimation skills by tracking actual vs. estimated hours for each task and adjusting my approach based on the data."
- **Technical breadth:** "I have been expanding my knowledge in [technology] through online courses and applying what I learn in side projects."
- **Delegation:** "I am learning to delegate more effectively. Last quarter, I started assigning specific sub-tasks to junior team members and providing guidance rather than doing everything myself."
- **Documentation:** "I have improved my documentation habits by blocking 30 minutes each Friday specifically for updating technical docs and READMEs."

# 4. RECEIVING FEEDBACK GRACEFULLY

How you respond to feedback says as much about you as the work itself. Stay calm, listen carefully, and respond professionally.

## 4.1. Positive Responses to Criticism

**When receiving constructive feedback:**
- "Thank you for that feedback. I appreciate you sharing this perspective."
- "That is helpful to hear. I was not fully aware of how that was perceived."
- "I understand your point. Can you give me a specific example so I can better understand the situation?"
- "I see. What would you have done differently in that situation?"
- "I appreciate the honesty. This gives me a clear direction for improvement."

**When you disagree with feedback (staying professional):**
- "I appreciate the feedback. I have a different perspective on this -- would it be okay to share my side?"
- "Thank you for raising this. I would like to provide some additional context that might be relevant."
- "I understand where you are coming from. In that particular instance, [context]. How would you suggest I handle similar situations in the future?"
- "I hear what you are saying. I think there may be some context I can add. Would that be helpful?"
- "That is fair feedback. I would love to discuss this further so I can fully understand your expectations."

## 4.2. Asking Clarifying Questions

- "Could you help me understand what specific behavior you would like to see more of?"
- "When you mention [area], could you point to a particular instance so I can reflect on it?"
- "What does success in this area look like from your perspective?"
- "Are there specific resources or training you would recommend for improving in this area?"
- "How would you prioritize the improvement areas we have discussed?"

# 5. SETTING GOALS FOR NEXT PERIOD

Performance reviews are forward-looking. Use this section to show ambition and a growth mindset.

## 5.1. SMART Goals Phrases

**Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound examples:**

- "By the end of Q2, I aim to earn the AWS Solutions Architect Associate certification."
- "Over the next six months, I plan to increase the test coverage of my primary module from 70% to 90%."
- "By the next review period, I want to lead at least two sprint planning sessions independently."
- "In the next quarter, I will reduce my average code review turnaround time to under 24 hours."
- "By December, I aim to complete three technical blog posts for the engineering team blog."

**Professional development goals:**
- "I plan to deepen my expertise in system design by studying architecture patterns and applying them in our upcoming projects."
- "I want to improve my mentoring skills by formally onboarding at least one new team member next quarter."
- "I aim to contribute to at least two open-source projects relevant to our tech stack this year."
- "I plan to improve my public speaking by presenting at one internal tech talk per quarter."
- "I intend to develop stronger product thinking by attending product strategy meetings and contributing to roadmap discussions."

## 5.2. Aligning Goals with Company Objectives

- "I understand that the company is focusing on scalability next year. I would like to contribute by deepening my knowledge of microservices architecture."
- "Since client satisfaction is a key metric, I plan to proactively gather and act on client feedback for my deliverables."
- "I want to support the team's goal of reducing technical debt by dedicating 20% of each sprint to refactoring."
- "In line with the company's growth plans, I would like to develop skills that prepare me for a technical lead role."
- "I aim to support the adoption of [new technology] by becoming an internal champion and helping the team transition."

# 6. ASKING FOR WHAT YOU WANT

Performance reviews are also an appropriate time to discuss your career trajectory and desires.

## 6.1. Promotion Path

- "I would like to discuss my career progression. What specific milestones do I need to achieve to be considered for a senior developer role?"
- "Based on my contributions this period, I believe I am ready for the next level. Could we discuss the promotion criteria?"
- "I am eager to take on more responsibility. What would you need to see from me to support a promotion?"
- "Could we create a concrete roadmap with timelines for my promotion to [role]?"
- "I have consistently met and exceeded expectations. I would like to understand the timeline for advancement to [level]."

## 6.2. New Responsibilities and Training

- "I would like to take on more architectural responsibilities in upcoming projects."
- "I am interested in leading the upcoming [project name] initiative. What would I need to demonstrate to earn that opportunity?"
- "I would appreciate the opportunity to attend [conference/training] to develop skills relevant to our team goals."
- "I would like to be more involved in client-facing discussions to develop my communication and consulting skills."
- "Could I shadow [senior colleague] on system design sessions to accelerate my learning?"

# 7. HANDLING DIFFICULT REVIEW CONVERSATIONS

Not every review goes as expected. Here is how to navigate tough situations.

## 7.1. When Rated Lower Than Expected

- "I appreciate the feedback. I was expecting a stronger rating based on [specific contributions]. Could we discuss what factors led to this assessment?"
- "I want to understand the gap between my self-assessment and the rating I received. Could we walk through the criteria together?"
- "Thank you for being candid. I would like to create a clear action plan so I can address these areas effectively."
- "I respect your assessment. Could you help me understand what specific improvements would make the biggest difference?"
- "I am committed to improving. Can we schedule a follow-up in 30 days to review my progress on these items?"

## 7.2. Disagreeing Respectfully

- "I would like to share a different perspective on that point, if I may."
- "I want to be transparent -- I see this situation differently. Here is my perspective..."
- "I understand your assessment. I would appreciate the chance to provide additional context that may not have been visible."
- "Could we look at this from a different angle? I have some data that might be relevant."
- "I respect your opinion. I would like to document my perspective as well so we have a balanced record."

## 7.3. When the Reviewer Is Vague

- "Could you give me a specific example of what you mean by 'needs improvement'?"
- "I want to make sure I understand. What does 'doing better' look like in concrete terms?"
- "When you say I should be 'more proactive,' could you describe a situation where you would have expected that?"
- "I appreciate the general direction. Could we break this down into two or three actionable items?"
- "Would it be possible to define specific success criteria so I know exactly what to work toward?"

# 8. FOLLOW-UP EMAIL TEMPLATES

## 8.1. Thank You Email After Review

```
Subject: Thank you - Performance Review Follow-Up

Hi [Manager's Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today for my performance review.
I appreciate the feedback and the thoughtful discussion about my contributions
and areas for growth.

I found our conversation about [specific topic discussed] particularly helpful,
and I am committed to working on the improvement areas we discussed.

I will send a follow-up with my action plan by [date]. Please let me know
if there is anything else you would like me to focus on.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## 8.2. Action Plan Email

```
Subject: Performance Review Action Plan

Hi [Manager's Name],

Following our review discussion, I have put together an action plan for the
next period:

1. [Goal 1] - Target completion: [date]
2. [Goal 2] - Target completion: [date]
3. [Goal 3] - Target completion: [date]

I would appreciate your feedback on these goals. If you would like to adjust
any of them, I am happy to discuss.

I also wanted to confirm our agreement on [specific item discussed]. I will
follow up on this by [date].

Thank you for your continued support and guidance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## 8.3. Commitment Email After Difficult Review

```
Subject: Commitment to Improvement - Follow-Up from Review

Hi [Manager's Name],

I want to thank you for the honest and constructive feedback during my review.
I take it seriously and am fully committed to addressing the areas we discussed.

Here is what I am committing to:

- [Specific action 1 with timeline]
- [Specific action 2 with timeline]
- [Specific action 3 with timeline]

I would like to schedule a check-in at [30/60 days] to review my progress.
Would [proposed date] work for you?

Thank you for your patience and for investing in my development.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## 8.4. Check-In Email Before Next Review

```
Subject: Pre-Review Check-In

Hi [Manager's Name],

As we approach my next review period, I wanted to share a brief summary
of my progress on the goals we set together:

1. [Goal 1] - [Status: completed / on track / needs attention]
2. [Goal 2] - [Status]
3. [Goal 3] - [Status]

I have also attached an updated self-assessment document for your review.

Would it be possible to schedule a brief check-in before the formal review
to discuss any preliminary feedback?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

# CONCLUSION

Performance reviews are not something to fear -- they are opportunities to showcase your value, align on expectations, and chart your career path. Prepare thoroughly by documenting your achievements with metrics, practice framing your improvement areas positively, and always follow up in writing. Remember that receiving feedback gracefully is a skill that distinguishes senior professionals. Use the templates and phrases in this guide to approach your next review with confidence and professionalism.
