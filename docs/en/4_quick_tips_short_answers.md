# QUICK TIPS & SHORT ANSWERS

## Grammar hacks, vocabulary tips, and short professional responses for daily communication

> **Purpose of this document**
>
> - Master short, natural-sounding responses to common questions
> - Learn quick grammar hacks to sound more native and polite
> - Discover vocabulary tips to avoid repetitive words
> - Respond confidently in chat (Slack/Teams) and spoken conversations

---

# 1. SHORT & PROFESSIONAL RESPONSES

Instead of always saying "Yes", "No", or "Okay", use these short phrases to sound more natural and engaged.

### When agreeing or confirming:

- **"Sounds good."** (Very common for confirming a plan or suggestion)
- **"Makes sense."** (When someone explains something to you)
- **"Will do."** (Short for "I will do that" - perfect for acknowledging a task)
- **"You got it."** (Friendly way to say "I will take care of your request")
- **"Exactly." / "Spot on."** (When someone correctly summarizes a point)

### When you need more time:

- **"Let me check."** (Instead of "Wait")
- **"I'll get back to you on that."** (When you don't know the answer immediately)
- **"Give me a sec."** (Informal, for chat or quick calls)

### When politely disagreeing or saying no:

- **"Not necessarily."** (When something isn't always true)
- **"I'm not so sure about that."** (Softer than "You are wrong")
- **"Let's hold off on that for now."** (Instead of "No, don't do it")

# 2. VOCABULARY HACKS: UPGRADING SIMPLE WORDS

Replace basic words with professional alternatives to elevate your communication.

### Instead of "Fix" -> Use "Resolve" or "Address"

- ❌ "I will fix the bug."
- ✅ "I will **resolve** the issue." or "Let's **address** this bug in the next sprint."

### Instead of "Change" -> Use "Modify", "Adjust", or "Update"

- ❌ "I changed the config file."
- ✅ "I **modified** the configuration." or "I **adjusted** the layout."

### Instead of "Make sure" -> Use "Ensure" or "Verify"

- ❌ "Make sure the server is running."
- ✅ "Please **ensure** the server is running." or "I will **verify** the deployment."

### Instead of "Tell me" -> Use "Let me know" or "Keep me posted"

- ❌ "Tell me when you finish."
- ✅ "Please **let me know** when it's done." or "**Keep me posted** on the progress."

# 3. GRAMMAR TIPS FOR POLITENESS

English relies heavily on grammar structures to soften requests and sound polite.

### Tip 1: Use Past Continuous for current soft requests

Using the past continuous makes a request sound like a gentle thought rather than a demand.

- 🔴 Direct: "I want to ask about the deadline."
- 🟢 Polite: "I **was wondering** if we could extend the deadline."
- 🟢 Polite: "I **was hoping** to get your feedback on this."

### Tip 2: Use "Could" or "Would" instead of "Can" or "Will"

- 🔴 Direct: "Can you send me the file?"
- 🟢 Polite: "**Could** you send me the file?"
- 🔴 Direct: "Will you join the meeting?"
- 🟢 Polite: "**Would** you be able to join the meeting?"

### Tip 3: Add modifiers to soften negative news

Words like _"a bit"_, _"slightly"_, or _"a little"_ make bad news easier to digest.

- 🔴 Direct: "We are behind schedule."
- 🟢 Polite: "We are running **a bit** behind schedule."
- 🔴 Direct: "There is a problem."
- 🟢 Polite: "There seems to be **a slight** issue."

# 4. CHAT ABBREVIATIONS & SLANG (SLACK/TEAMS)

Common acronyms used in modern IT workplaces:

- **LGTM**: Looks Good To Me (Used in code reviews to approve a PR)
- **WIP**: Work In Progress
- **TBD**: To Be Determined (Decided later)
- **ETA**: Estimated Time of Arrival (When will a task be done? e.g., "What's the ETA on this feature?")
- **OOO**: Out Of Office (e.g., "I'm OOO tomorrow")
- **TL;DR**: Too Long; Didn't Read (Used to give a quick summary of a long message)
- **FYI**: For Your Information
