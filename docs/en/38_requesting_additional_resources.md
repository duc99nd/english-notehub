# Requesting Additional Resources

As a lead developer or project manager, you may find that your team is overloaded, the timeline is too aggressive, or the project requires a specialized skill (e.g., security, DevOps, or UI design) that the team lacks. Requesting additional budget, developers, or specialists requires a data-driven approach, showing that the request is necessary for project success.

## 1. Stating the Need for More Resources

Explain clearly that the current capacity is insufficient to meet the project goals.

- "To deliver the project on schedule, we need to add another backend developer to the team."
- "Given our current commitments, our existing developers are at 100% capacity. We cannot take on the billing integration without additional support."
- "We need to request a dedicated QA engineer for the next three weeks to handle the regression testing."

## 2. Providing Data-Driven Rationale (The "Why")

Do not just ask for people; explain *why* they are needed. Use data such as velocity, burndown charts, or skill gaps.

- "Our current team velocity is 25 story points per sprint, but the remaining backlog requires an average velocity of 35 points to meet the launch date."
- "The integration of the mapping API requires specialized GIS knowledge that our current developers do not possess."
- "Due to the recent expansion of the project scope, we have accumulated 150 hours of additional work that was not in our original estimate."

## 3. Highlighting the Risk of Not Adding Resources

Explain the consequences if your request is rejected.

- "If we do not add another frontend developer, we will be forced to push back our release date by at least three weeks."
- "Without a dedicated security auditor, we risk releasing the application with potential vulnerabilities, which would violate our compliance standards."
- "Asking the team to work overtime to cover this gap will lead to burnout and increase the likelihood of introducing critical bugs."

## 4. Proposing a Realistic Plan/Solution

Offer options on how to get the resources (e.g., hiring a contractor, borrowing a developer from another team, or hiring a freelancer).

- "I propose borrowing a database administrator from Team B for just 10 hours next week to help optimize our database schemas."
- "We can bring in a contract developer for two months to help us get through this peak workload phase."
- "Alternatively, if hiring is not an option, we must reduce the project scope and cut the reporting module from this release."

## 5. Safe Templates to Present to Management

- **Requesting a developer from another team:** "I’d like to request some temporary assistance from the DevOps team. We need about 15 hours of support next week to configure our Kubernetes clusters for staging. Without this, our testing phase will be delayed."
- **Requesting to hire/contract a developer:** "To meet the December launch date for the e-commerce portal, we need to increase our team capacity. I suggest hiring a contract full-stack developer for a 3-month period to focus entirely on the API integrations. This will allow our core team to focus on the UI and database optimization."
