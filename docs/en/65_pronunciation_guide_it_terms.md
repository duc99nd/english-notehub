## 5. Tricky Pairs (Words That Sound Similar)

Be careful not to confuse these pairs in meetings:

1.  **"cache" /k√¶ É/  vs. "catch" /k√¶t É/:** "Let me clear the **cache**" ‚Č† "Let me **catch** the bug."
2.  **"route" /ruňźt/ vs. "root" /ruňźt/:** In US English, both sound the same. In UK English, "route" is /ra ät/.
3.  **"merge" /m…úňźrd í/ vs. "much" /m Ćt É/:** "Please **merge** the PR" ‚ÄĒ not "Please **much** the PR."
4.  **"queue" /kjuňź/ vs. "query" /ňąkw…™ri/:** "Add a task to the **queue**" vs. "Run a database **query**."
5.  **"launch" /l…Ēňźnt É/ vs. "lunch" /l Ćnt É/:** "We will **launch** the app" ‚ÄĒ not "We will **lunch** the app."
6.  **"log" /l…í…°/ vs. "lock" /l…ík/:** "Check the server **logs**" vs. "The database is **locked**."
7.  **"height" /ha…™t/ vs. "width" /w…™dőł/:** Both are CSS properties. Note: "height" has a silent "gh".
8.  **"pull" /p äl/ vs. "pool" /puňźl/:** "Create a **pull** request" vs. "Configure the connection **pool**."
9.  **"live" /la…™v/ (adj) vs. "live" /l…™v/ (verb):** "The site is **live** /la…™v/" vs. "I **live** /l…™v/ in Hanoi."
10. **"access" /ňą√¶ks…õs/ vs. "assess" /…ôňąs…õs/:** "Grant **access** to the server" vs. "**Assess** the risk."

## 6. Stress Patterns for Multi-Syllable Words

In English, stressing the wrong syllable can make a word completely unintelligible. Here are common IT words with their correct stress marked in **CAPS**:

1.  **de-VEL-op-er** (not DEV-el-oper)
2.  **ap-pli-CA-tion** (not ap-PLI-cation)
3.  **au-then-ti-CA-tion** (not au-THEN-tication)
4.  **con-fig-u-RA-tion** (not CON-figuration)
5.  **do-cu-men-TA-tion** (not DOC-umentation)
6.  **im-ple-men-TA-tion** (not IM-plementation)
7.  **in-fra-STRUC-ture** (not infra-struc-TURE)
8.  **re-POS-i-to-ry** (not REP-ository)
9.  **ar-chi-TEC-ture** (not ARCH-itecture)
10. **vul-ner-a-BIL-i-ty** (not vul-NER-ability)
