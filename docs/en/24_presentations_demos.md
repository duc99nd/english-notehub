# PRESENTATIONS & PRODUCT DEMOS (WEB APPS)

## Essential phrases for greeting clients, asking questions, and speaking while interacting with web apps

> **How to use this document**
> - **Highlighted Sections 1 & 2** are placed at the top for immediate access when you need to demo a web app and talk at the same time.
> - Use these templates to reduce hesitation pauses ("uhm", "ah") during live demonstrations.
> - Focus on short, actionable phrases that match your mouse actions.

---

# 1. LIVE WEB DEMO: SPEAKING WHILE INTERACTING
Use these expressions to describe exactly what you are doing in real-time as you navigate the website or application.

### 1.1. Clicking, Hovering, and Scrolling
* **Clicking:**
  * *"Now, I'm going to click on the 'Submit' button."*
  * *"Let's click here to proceed to the next step."*
  * *"If I click this link, it will redirect us to the billing page."*
* **Hovering:**
  * *"As I hover over this card, you'll see a tooltip showing the details."*
  * *"Hovering over this button changes the state to active."*
* **Scrolling:**
  * *"Let me scroll down to show you the footer section."*
  * *"If we scroll up, the header sticks to the top of the page."*

### 1.2. Filling Forms and Entering Data
* **Entering input:**
  * *"I'll type in a test email address here: `test@example.com`."*
  * *"Let me fill in the required fields with some sample data."*
  * *"I am entering a random value to test the validation."*
* **Selecting options:**
  * *"I'll select 'Credit Card' from this dropdown menu."*
  * *"Let's check this box to accept the terms."*
  * *"I am toggling this switch to enable dark mode."*

### 1.3. Describing UI Changes & Screen States
* **Redirecting & Loading:**
  * *"The page is loading now. It should take just a second."*
  * *"Please bear with me for a moment while the server processes the request."*
  * *"Now the screen has refreshed, and we are on the dashboard."*
* **Modals, Popups, and Alerts:**
  * *"Clicking this trigger opens up a confirmation modal."*
  * *"As you can see, a success notification pops up in the top-right corner."*
  * *"Let's close this modal by clicking the 'X' button or clicking outside."*
* **Pointing out UI elements:**
  * *"If you look at the sidebar on the left, you'll see the navigation menu."*
  * *"Notice the new red badge showing the pending count."*
  * *"At the bottom of this table, we have implemented pagination."*

# 2. GREETING & INTERACTING WITH CLIENTS

### 2.1. Welcoming the Client
* *"Hi [Client Name], thank you for joining our demo today."*
* *"Good morning/afternoon everyone. It's great to have you all here."*
* *"Hi team, today I'll be presenting the latest updates on the web platform."*

### 2.2. Setting the Rules for Questions
* **Allowing interruptions:**
  * *"Please feel free to stop me at any point if you have questions."*
  * *"If you want to discuss anything in detail as we go, just let me know."*
* **Saving questions for the end:**
  * *"I'll run through the demo first, and then we'll open the floor for questions."*
  * *"To keep things on track, I'll take all questions at the end of the presentation."*

### 2.3. Asking the Client Questions & Seeking Feedback (During the Demo)
Keep the client engaged and verify if the implementation matches their expectation.
* **General checks:**
  * *"How does this look to you so far?"*
  * *"Does this layout match what you had in mind?"*
  * *"Is this the expected behavior you wanted for this button?"*
* **Choosing options:**
  * *"Would you prefer this modal to close automatically, or should it stay open?"*
  * *"Should we display this information on the main screen, or hide it behind a hover?"*
* **Verification:**
  * *"Does this flow make sense, or should we simplify it?"*
  * *"Would you like me to walk through this specific step again?"*

---

# 3. OPENING THE DEMO & SETTING THE GOAL
State what you are going to present before sharing your screen.

* *"Today, I'd like to walk you through the new dashboard feature."*
* *"In this sprint, our team focused on optimizing the performance of the checkout flow."*
* *"The goal of today's demo is to showcase the integration with the new payment gateway."*
* *"Before I share my screen, let me give you a quick overview of what we built."*

---

# 4. TRANSITIONS & PROGRESSION
Keep the flow smooth when jumping between features.

* *"Let's move on to the next feature, which is the reporting system."*
* *"Now, if we navigate to the settings tab, we can configure the notifications."*
* *"Moving forward, I'll show you how the admin handles this user request."*
* *"To demonstrate this, I'm going to create a dummy account and run a test transaction."*

---

# 5. BUSINESS VALUE & HIGHLIGHTS
Connect technical work to business outcomes.

* *"One of the key benefits of this update is that it reduces load times by 50%."*
* *"This feature will significantly improve the user onboarding experience."*
* *"By doing this, we eliminate the need for manual data entry, saving time for the admin."*
* *"What's great about this implementation is that it's highly scalable for future features."*

---

# 6. HANDLING DEMO GLITCHES (LIVE BUGS)
Remain calm and professional if something breaks.

* *"It seems we are experiencing a slight technical hiccup. Let me refresh the page."*
* *"This worked perfectly during our staging tests. I'll look into why it is failing here right after the meeting."*
* *"Let's skip this specific flow for now and come back to it if we have time."*
* *"There appears to be a delay with the staging server right now, but you get the general idea."*

---

# 7. WRAP-UP & Q&A
Close the meeting and gather final feedback.

* *"That brings me to the end of my demo. To summarize, we have implemented..."*
* *"Thank you for your time. I'd be happy to take any questions you might have."*

### Handling Difficult Q&A Questions:
* *"That's a great question. From a technical perspective, yes, we can do that."*
* *"I don't have the exact performance numbers right now, but I will find out and get back to you today."*
* *"We haven't considered that specific edge case yet, but it's a valid point. I'll add it to the backlog."*
