# Job Interview Preparation (Daily English Edition)

For many developers, speaking English in an interview is scary. But you do **not** need complex, academic vocabulary. You just need clear, natural, and simple daily English. This guide is written for both beginners and experienced developers to help you speak with confidence using everyday phrases.

---

## 1. Academic vs. Daily English (Comparison)

Stop trying to sound like a textbook. Keep your sentences short and direct.

| ❌ Academic (Too formal, hard to pronounce) | 🟢 Daily & Natural (Easy to say, friendly) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "I am responsible for the implementation of new functionalities." | "I build new features for the app." |
| "Having observed your corporation's expansion in fintech..." | "I saw your company on LinkedIn, and I really like your product." |
| "We encountered a critical performance degradation issue." | "Our system became very slow." |
| "I utilize Node.js for backend development purposes." | "I use Node.js for the backend." |
| "I executed the migration of our database schemas." | "I moved our database to a new version." |

## 2. Tell Me About Yourself (Simple Pitch)

When the interviewer says: *"Tell me about yourself,"* do not repeat your CV. Use the **Present-Past-Future** recipe. Use simple sentences.

### 💡 Fill-in-the-blank Template:
*   **Present:** "Hi, my name is **[Your Name]**. Currently, I work as a **[Your Role, e.g., Frontend Developer]** at **[Company]**. I use **[Tech Stack, e.g., React and TypeScript]** daily."
*   **Past:** "Before this, I worked at **[Old Company]** for **[Number]** years. I built a **[Project name / feature, e.g., checkout page]** there. It was a great experience because **[simple reason, e.g., I learned a lot about APIs]**."
*   **Future:** "Now, I want to grow my career. I want to join a product team where I can use my skills. I saw this role at your company, and I think it is a great match for me."

### 🗣️ Example Pitch (Easy to speak):
> *"Hi, my name is Duc. I am a Frontend Developer with 3 years of experience. Right now, I work at a startup in Hanoi. I build user interfaces using React and Tailwind CSS. In my last job, I worked on an e-commerce website. I helped the team make the checkout page faster. Now, I want to challenge myself in a larger team. I saw your job posting on Facebook, and I really like your team culture. That is why I am here today."*

## 3. Speaking in "STAR" Method (Behavioral Questions)

When they ask about a past situation (e.g., *"Tell me about a time you made a mistake"*), use short steps.

### Situation (The Problem) - 2 sentences max
- "In my last project, we had a big bug on production."
- "Our app crashed when users clicked the payment button."

### Task (Your Job) - 1 sentence
- "My job was to find the bug and fix it quickly."
- "My team lead asked me to resolve this issue in two hours."

### Action (What you did) - 3-4 simple sentences
Use **"First"**, **"Then"**, **"After that"** to make it easy to follow.
- "**First**, I checked the server logs."
- "**Then**, I found out that the API was returning a null value."
- "**After that**, I wrote a simple check to prevent the crash."
- "**Finally**, I pushed the hotfix to production."

### Result (The Happy Ending) - 1 sentence
- "The app was back online in 15 minutes, and users were happy."
- "We saved the company from losing sales."

## 4. Daily Q&A Templates

### Q1: What are your strengths? (Điểm mạnh)
*   **Easy Formula:** "I think my biggest strength is **[Strength]**. For example, **[Simple story]**."
*   **Example:** "I think my biggest strength is **fast learning**. For example, last month my team needed to use Vue.js for a new project. I had never used it before. But I read the official docs, watched a few tutorials, and was able to write code in just three days."

### Q2: What is your weakness? (Điểm yếu)
*   **Easy Formula:** "Sometimes I struggle with **[Weakness]**. But I am trying to improve by **[Action]**."
*   **Example:** "Sometimes I struggle with **saying no**. When teammates ask for help, I always say yes, and then I get too busy. But now, I am using a calendar to manage my time better. If I am too busy, I politely ask them to wait."

### Q3: Why are you leaving your current job? (Tại sao chuyển việc)
Keep it positive. Focus on learning new things.
- **Example:** "I really like my current team, but the project is quite small now. I want to learn how to build larger systems with high traffic. Your company is growing fast, and I want to be a part of that."

---

## 5. Daily Questions to Ask the Interviewer

At the end, ask 2-3 simple questions to show you care.

*   "Can you tell me about the daily tasks of this role?" (Công việc hàng ngày là gì?)
*   "What technologies does the team use daily?" (Team đang dùng công nghệ gì hàng ngày?)
*   "How does the team handle code reviews?" (Team review code như thế nào?)
*   "What are the next steps in this interview process?" (Các bước tiếp theo là gì?)

---

## 🗣️ Speaking Secrets for Newbies
1.  **Don't rush (Đừng nói quá nhanh):** Speak slowly. It gives your brain time to choose the next word.
2.  **Short is safe (Ngắn cho an toàn):** If you make a sentence too long, you will make grammar mistakes. Keep it under 12 words per sentence.
3.  **Use linking words (Dùng từ nối đơn giản):** Use `Actually`, `Basically`, `Personally`, `To be honest` at the start of sentences to sound natural.
