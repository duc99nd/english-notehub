# ENGLISH MEETING TEMPLATES FOR TEAM LEAD

## Safe speaking formulas for weekly client meetings

> **Purpose of this document**
>
> - Help you confidently lead weekly meetings with clients
> - Use simple, clear, natural English
> - Ready-made templates — just replace keywords each week
> - Know what to say when: you know the answer, you're unsure, you can't hear, the client needs to explain more, or you need to follow up after the meeting

> **How to use this document**
>
> - Learn in groups of sentences
> - No need to memorize everything at once
> - Each week, just replace `[task]`, `[issue]`, `[date]`, `[time]`, `[expected behavior]`
> - Prioritize short, clear, natural sentences

---

# 1. SAFE SPEAKING MINDSET IN WEEKLY MEETINGS

## 1.1. Speaking Goals

You don't need to speak "perfect" English. You just need to:

- keep it short
- keep it clear
- follow the flow
- use a few safe formulas
- know how to ask for repetition / confirmation / follow up

## 1.2. Key Principles

- No need to sound academic
- Prioritize short + clear + natural sentences
- Speaking slowly is better than speaking too long
- If unsure, don't guess
- If stuck, go back to simple formulas

## 1.3. Ultra-Safe Formulas

- **We completed...**
- **We're working on...**
- **We'll focus on...**
- **We found an issue related to...**
- **Could you clarify...?**
- **Let me double-check and get back to you.**
- **If there are no more questions, we can wrap up here.**

# 2. COMPLETE WEEKLY MEETING FLOW

A typical weekly meeting follows this order:

1. Greetings
2. Opening the meeting
3. State the meeting goal
4. Done
5. Doing
6. Next
7. Issue / blocker / risk
8. Confirm / ask the client
9. Q&A
10. Summarize action items
11. Closing / schedule next meeting

# 3. FULL WEEKLY MEETING SCRIPTS: 3 / 5 / 10 MINUTES

## 3.1. Full script — 3 minutes

> **Use when:** Short weekly meeting, quick update, few issues.

### 🟢 Greetings

- Hi everyone, good morning.
- I hope you're doing well.
- Thanks for joining today's meeting.

### 📋 Agenda

- Today, I'd like to give a quick update on the project status.
- I'll walk you through what we completed last week, what we're currently working on, and what's next.

### ✅ Done

- Last week, we completed [task 1] and [task 2].
- We also fixed [bug/issue] from the previous round of testing.

### 🔄 Doing

- Currently, we're working on [task 3].
- At the moment, the team is also focusing on [task 4 / integration / internal QA].

### ⏭️ Next

- Next, we'll focus on [task 5].
- We're planning to complete [task 6] by [day / end of this week].

### ⚠️ Issue

- We also found one issue related to [issue].
- The team is already looking into it, and we'll keep you updated on the progress.

### ❓ Confirm

- Before we move forward, we'd like to confirm [point] with you.
- Could you clarify the expected behavior for [case]?

### 💬 Q&A

- That's the update from our side.
- Do you have any questions or concerns so far?

### 👋 Wrap-up

- If there are no more questions, we can wrap up here.
- We'll follow up on the open items after the meeting.
- Thank you very much for your time today. See you next week at the same time.

## 3.2. Full script — 5 minutes

> **Use when:** Standard weekly meeting with Done / Doing / Next / Issue / Confirm.

### 🟢 Greetings

- Hi everyone, good morning.
- I hope you're doing well and had a good week.
- Thanks for joining today's meeting.

### 📋 Agenda

- Today, I'd like to give a quick update on the project status.
- I'll go through last week's progress, the current status, the next steps, and a few open points that we'd like to confirm with you.

### ✅ Done

- First, let me start with what we completed last week.
- Last week, we completed [task 1], [task 2], and [task 3].
- We also finished testing for [feature/module] and resolved several issues related to [module / flow / bug type].
- From our side, these items are now completed.

### 🔄 Doing

- Currently, we're working on [task 4] and [task 5].
- At the moment, the team is focusing on [priority], especially around [integration / validation / bug fixing / internal QA].
- We're also reviewing [feedback / requirements] from the previous discussion and updating the solution accordingly.

### ⏭️ Next

- For the next steps, we'll focus on [task 6].
- We're also planning to complete [task 7] by [time].
- Once this is done, we'll move on to [task 8].

### ⚠️ Issue

- There is one issue we'd like to raise today.
- We found an issue related to [issue].
- At the moment, the team is investigating the root cause and working on a solution.
- This may affect [timeline / testing / scope] slightly, but we're doing our best to minimize the impact.

### ❓ Confirm

- We'd also like to confirm one point with you before moving forward.
- Could you clarify the expected behavior for [case]?
- We want to make sure we understand the requirement correctly before implementation.
- If I understand correctly, the expected flow is [your understanding], right?

### 💬 Q&A

- That's the update from our side for today.
- Do you have any questions, concerns, or feedback so far?
- If needed, I can go into more detail on any item.

### 📝 Action Items

- Before we wrap up, let me quickly summarize the action items.
- **From our side:** We'll continue working on [item]. We'll also follow up on [issue / open question].
- **From your side:** We'll wait for confirmation on [point].

### 👋 Wrap-up

- If there are no more questions, we can wrap up here.
- Thank you very much for your time today.
- We'll follow up via chat after the meeting and see you next [day] at [time].

## 3.3. Full script — 10 minutes

> **Use when:** Meeting with many topics, issues to discuss, logic to present, or alignment needed with client.

### 🟢 Greetings

- Hi everyone, good morning.
- I hope you're doing well.
- Thank you for joining today's weekly sync.

### 📋 Agenda

- Today, I'd like to give a structured update on the project.
- I'll walk you through what we completed last week, what we're currently working on, what we plan to do next, and a few issues and confirmation points that we'd like to discuss with you.

### ✅ Done — Last week

- First, let me start with the progress from last week.
- Over the past week, the team completed [task 1], [task 2], and [task 3].
- We also finished [testing / implementation / review] for [feature/module].
- In addition, we resolved several remaining issues related to [module / business flow / bug type].
- From our side, those items are now considered completed.
- We also completed [task 4], which was important for preparing the next phase. This gives us a better foundation for [next phase / integration / testing / deployment].

### 🔄 Doing — Current status

- Currently, we're working on [task 5], [task 6], and [task 7].
- At the moment, the team is mainly focusing on [priority].
- This includes [bug fixing / integration / validating business logic / internal QA / reviewing client feedback].
- We're also reviewing [requirement / scenario / design / business rule] to make sure the implementation matches your expectation.
- As of now, the overall progress is still on track, although there is one point we'd like to discuss more carefully.

### ⏭️ Next — Upcoming work

- This week, our main focus is to complete [task 8] and continue with [task 9].
- After that, we plan to move on to [task 10].
- If everything goes as expected, we should be able to finish [deliverable] by [day / milestone date].

### ⚠️ Issue — Current problem

- We found an issue related to [issue].
- More specifically, it affects [flow / module / user scenario].
- At the moment, the team is still investigating the root cause.
- Based on what we know so far, the issue may be related to [logic / data sync / API response / third-party behavior / unclear requirement].
- This may affect [timeline / testing / user flow] slightly, but we're already working on a solution and doing our best to keep the impact as small as possible.

### ❓ Confirm — Alignment needed

- Before we proceed, we'd like to align with you on one important point.
- Could you clarify the expected behavior for [case / scenario]?
- We want to make sure we fully understand the business expectation before we finalize the implementation.

### 🧠 Explain — Our current understanding

- Let me explain our current understanding.
- In simple terms, [A] happens first, then [B], and then [C].
- For example, if [case 1], the system should [result 1]. If [case 2], the system should [result 2].
- So based on our current understanding, the expected result should be [result].
- If that understanding is correct, we'll continue with the current approach.
- If not, we're happy to adjust based on your feedback.
- Also, if there are any concerns from your side about [feature / timeline / expected behavior], please let us know.

### 💬 Q&A

- That's the main update from our side.
- Do you have any questions or concerns so far?

**If you understand the question:**

- Yes, in this case, the expected behavior is [behavior].
- According to the current logic, the system should [result].
- From our understanding, this should be handled as [solution].

**If you're not fully sure:**

- Let me double-check this point first.
- I'd like to confirm this with the team before I answer.
- I don't want to give you the wrong information, so let me verify it first.

**If it's too technical or complex:**

- This point is a bit complex, so I'll follow up with a detailed answer via Google Chat after the meeting.
- I'd prefer to confirm this internally first and send you a clear written update.

### 📝 Action Items

- Before we wrap up, let me summarize the action items.
- **From our side:**
  - We'll continue with [task / issue handling / validation]
  - We'll check [open point]
  - We'll follow up on [technical question / issue]
- **From your side:**
  - We'd appreciate confirmation on [requirement / expected behavior / option A vs option B]

### 👋 Wrap-up

- If there are no more questions, we can wrap up here.
- Thank you very much for your time and feedback today.
- We'll follow up on the open items after the meeting and see you again on [day] at [time].

## 4.11. Explaining Business Logic / Domain Knowledge

### Safe Presentation Approach

Follow 4 steps:

1. State the goal briefly
2. Explain the flow simply
3. Give an example
4. Confirm the expected result

### 🛡️ Safe Formulas

- **Let me explain the business logic behind this**
- **In simple terms**
- **Basically**
- **For example**
- **So the expected result is**

### 💡 Examples

1. **Let me explain the business logic behind this part.**
2. **In simple terms, [A] happens first, then [B], and finally [C].**
3. **Basically, the system checks [condition] before allowing [action].**
4. **This is designed to make sure that [business purpose].**
5. **For example, if [case], then the system will [result].**
6. **If [condition A] is true, we follow flow A. Otherwise, we follow flow B.**
7. **The reason we handle it this way is [reason].**
8. **From a business perspective, this rule helps prevent [risk/problem].**
9. **So the expected result in this scenario is [result].**
10. **Please let me know if you'd like me to explain this with another example.**

## 4.21. Rescheduling / Canceling / Skipping a Meeting

> **Key vocabulary**
>
> - **reschedule** = move to a different time
> - **cancel** = cancel that session
> - **postpone** = delay
> - **put it on hold** = pause temporarily
> - **skip next week's meeting** = skip one session

### 💡 Examples

1. **I may be unavailable next week. Could we reschedule the meeting?**
2. **Would it be possible to move the meeting to another time next week?**
3. **Could we postpone next week's meeting?**
4. **Would it be okay to skip next week's meeting and continue the week after?**
5. **I have a scheduling conflict next week, so I'd like to reschedule our meeting.**
6. **If needed, we can cancel next week's meeting and follow up via chat instead.**
7. **Would you be okay if we put this meeting on hold for one week?**
8. **Next week is a bit busy on our side. Could we move the meeting to [day/time]?**
9. **Can we pause the regular meeting for next week only?**
10. **Please let me know if rescheduling works for you.**

- **Please let us know if you agree with this approach.**

This shows strong leadership and initiative.

### 💡 Examples

- **So, from our side, we'll update [task/item].**
- **From your side, we'll wait for confirmation on [item].**
- **We'll follow up on this after the meeting.**
- **Let me summarize the next actions briefly.**

## 5.4. Managing Meeting Time

When the client goes off-topic, bring them back:

### 💡 Examples

- **To keep us on track, let's focus on this item first.**
- **We can come back to that in a moment.**
- **Let's finish this point first, then we can discuss that.**
- **That's a good point, and we'll cover it shortly.**

## 5.5. "Safety Net" Phrases

When unsure, don't guess.

### 💡 Examples

- **Let me double-check that and get back to you.**
- **I'd like to confirm this internally first.**
- **I don't want to give you incorrect information.**
- **I'll follow up via chat after the meeting.**

# 6. KEY GRAMMAR POINTS FOR MEETINGS

## 6.1. Present Simple

### When to Use

- Stating general facts
- Describing processes
- Explaining business logic or rules

### Formula

- **Subject + verb**

### 💡 Examples

- **The system checks the order status first.**
- **The user selects the payment method.**
- **This process starts when the request is approved.**

### Common Use Cases

- Explaining business flows
- Describing domain logic
- Explaining how the system works

---

## 6.2. Present Continuous

### When to Use

- Talking about current ongoing work
- Describing what the team is doing now

### Formula

- **Subject + am/is/are + V-ing**

### 💡 Examples

- **We're working on the payment module.**
- **The team is fixing the remaining bugs.**
- **We're reviewing the latest feedback from your side.**

### Common Use Cases

- The "Doing" section
- Current progress updates

---

## 6.3. Present Perfect

### When to Use

- Reporting completed work that's still relevant
- Sharing results of finished tasks

### Formula

- **Subject + have/has + past participle**

### 💡 Examples

- **We've completed the login module.**
- **We've finished the main development tasks.**
- **We've already fixed that issue.**

### Common Use Cases

- The "Done" section
- Reporting last week's results

> **Quick Reference**
>
> - **Done** → use **have completed / have finished**
> - **Doing** → use **are working on**
> - **Next** → use **will / plan to**

---

## 6.4. Future: will / plan to / going to

### When to Use

- Talking about next steps
- Describing upcoming plans
- Making projections

### 💡 Examples

- **We'll continue with integration next week.**
- **We plan to complete testing by Friday.**
- **We're going to start UAT next week.**

### Quick Guide

- **will** = decisions / predictions / promises
- **plan to** = more concrete plans
- **going to** = intentions already decided

### Common Phrases

- **Next, we'll focus on [task].**
- **We plan to complete [task] this week.**
- **We're going to start the next phase on Monday.**

---

## 6.5. Modal Verbs: can / could / should / may / would

These are essential for sounding polite and natural.

### can

- Ability / capability

**Examples**

- **We can complete this by Friday.**
- **We can support that change.**

### could

- More polite when asking
- Softer suggestions

**Examples**

- **Could you clarify this point?**
- **Could you repeat that, please?**

### should

- Suggestions / recommendations

**Examples**

- **We should review this logic again.**
- **This should be handled in the backend.**

### may

- Possibility
- Softer, safer tone

**Examples**

- **This may affect the timeline slightly.**
- **There may be some impact on testing.**

### would

- Polite requests
- Softer tone

**Examples**

- **I'd like to give a quick update.**
- **We'd like to confirm this point with you.**
- **Would you mind explaining this scenario?**

---

## 6.6. Simple Conditionals

### When to Use

- Expressing conditions
- Explaining logic
- If A, then B scenarios

### Common Patterns

- **If + present simple, ...**
- **If this happens, we will...**
- **If the user selects [A], the system shows [B].**

### 💡 Examples

- **If the user enters invalid data, the system shows an error message.**
- **If we get your confirmation today, we'll start development tomorrow.**
- **If this issue continues, we may need more time.**

---

## 6.7. Passive Voice

### When to Use

- Focusing on the result rather than who did it
- Professional, concise reporting

### 💡 Examples

- **The issue has been fixed.**
- **The feature was deployed yesterday.**
- **The report will be shared after the meeting.**

### Common Use Cases

- Status reports
- Closing tasks
- Professional, concise communication

---

## 6.8. Linking Words / Transitions

These are crucial for smooth meeting flow.

### Most Common Transitions

- **First,**
- **Next,**
- **Also,**
- **In addition,**
- **However,**
- **At the moment,**
- **For example,**
- **So,**
- **That's why...**

### 💡 Examples

- **First, we completed the UI update.**
- **Next, we moved to backend integration.**
- **However, we found one issue in the payment flow.**
- **So, we'd like to confirm the expected behavior with you.**

# 7. SPEAKING NATURALLY AND CLEARLY

## 7.1. Don't Translate Word-by-Word

Aim for short, friendly, natural-sounding sentences.

### Avoid

- **Today I want to present about the tasks of the previous week.**

### Better

- **Today, I'd like to give a quick update on last week's progress.**

---

## 7.2. Use Familiar Verbs

Prioritize these safe, common verbs:

- **finish**
- **complete**
- **work on**
- **review**
- **check**
- **confirm**
- **update**
- **discuss**
- **clarify**
- **follow up**

---

## 7.3. Use Softer Phrases for Politeness

### Instead of

- **You need to explain this.**

### Say

- **Could you help explain this part?**
- **Could you clarify this point for us?**

---

## 7.4. Keep Sentences Short

### No need for

- overly long sentences with multiple clauses

### Better

- **We finished the API.**
- **We're testing the flow now.**
- **We found one issue.**
- **We need your confirmation on this part.**

---

## 7.5. Use Softening Phrases

These make you sound more natural:

- **I think...**
- **At the moment...**
- **From our side...**
- **As far as I know...**
- **From our understanding...**
- **It seems that...**
- **Just to make sure...**
- **To keep things simple...**

### 💡 Examples

- **From our understanding, this flow should start after approval.**
- **Just to make sure, you'd like us to update the report format as well, right?**
- **At the moment, we're still checking that point.**

---

## 7.6. Unnatural vs. Natural — Common Comparisons

### 1. Weekly Report

- **Unnatural:** I want to present about last week tasks.
- **Natural:** I'd like to give a quick update on last week's progress.

### 2. Asking for Questions

- **Unnatural:** Do you have QA?
- **Natural:** Do you have any questions or concerns so far?

### 3. Requesting an Explanation

- **Unnatural:** Please explain again.
- **Natural:** Could you explain that one more time, please?

### 4. When You Don't Know

- **Unnatural:** I don't know.
- **Natural:** I'm not sure about that at the moment. Let me check and get back to you.

### 5. Following Up

- **Unnatural:** I will answer later by chat.
- **Natural:** I'll follow up with a clearer answer via Google Chat after the meeting.

# 8. SAFE FORMULAS — THE SAFEST SPEAKING PATTERNS

## 8.1. Progress Reporting

- **Last week, we completed [task].**
- **Currently, we're working on [task].**
- **Next, we'll focus on [task].**

## 8.2. Talking About Issues

- **We found an issue related to [topic].**
- **The team is already looking into it.**
- **This may affect [timeline/testing/scope].**

## 8.3. Requesting Confirmation

- **We'd like to confirm [point] with you.**
- **Could you confirm whether [A] or [B] is correct?**
- **Please let us know if this direction works for you.**

## 8.4. When You Can't Hear Clearly

- **Sorry, could you repeat that?**
- **Sorry, I didn't catch that clearly.**
- **Could you speak a little more slowly, please?**

## 8.5. Confirming Your Understanding

- **If I understand correctly, you mean [idea], right?**
- **Just to make sure I understood correctly...**
- **So you'd like us to [action], correct?**

## 8.6. When Unsure / Don't Know

- **Let me double-check that first.**
- **I'd like to confirm this internally.**
- **I don't want to give you the wrong information.**
- **I'll get back to you after the meeting.**

## 8.7. Following Up via Chat

- **This point is a bit complex, so I'll follow up via Google Chat after the meeting.**
- **I'll send you a more detailed answer in writing.**
- **Let me confirm this with the team and reply via chat.**
