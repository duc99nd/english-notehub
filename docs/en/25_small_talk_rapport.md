# ENGLISH SMALL TALK AND RAPPORT

## Natural English for opening meetings, building rapport, and ending conversations smoothly

> **Purpose of this document**
>
> - Speak more naturally at the start and end of meetings
> - Feel friendlier while staying professional
> - Covers weekly meetings, first meetings, informal catch-ups, and short conversations with clients
> - Safe templates — not too casual, but still easy to listen to

> **How to use this document**
>
> - Choose the right level for your audience
> - If you don't know them well, use the "safe and professional" group
> - If you've worked together for a while, use the "warmer and more natural" group
> - You don't need to say a lot — 1 to 2 sentences is enough

---

# 1. SAFE SMALL TALK MINDSET

## 1.1. The Goal

Small talk at work doesn't need to be long.

It just needs to be:

- friendly
- light
- not too personal
- easy to transition into the main topic

## 1.2. Safe Topics

- today / start of the week
- weather (very brief)
- work schedule
- general progress
- thanking people for joining

## 1.3. Topics to Avoid (With People You Don't Know Well)

- overly personal matters
- politics
- money
- private comments

---

# 2. NATURAL OPENING GREETINGS

## 2.1. Safe and Professional

- **Hi everyone, good morning.**
- **Hello everyone, I hope you're doing well.**
- **Good afternoon, everyone. Thanks for joining.**

## 2.2. Warmer and More Natural

- **Hi everyone, hope you're having a good day so far.**
- **Good morning, everyone. It's good to see you again.**
- **Hello everyone, I hope your week is going well.**

---

# 3. START OF THE WEEK / START OF THE DAY

## 3.1. Monday Openers

- **Happy Monday, everyone. I hope you had a good weekend.**
- **I hope everyone had a nice weekend.**

## 3.2. Mid-Week

- **I hope your week is going smoothly.**
- **Hope everything is going well on your side.**

## 3.3. Start of the Day

- **I hope your day is off to a good start.**
- **Hope you're doing well today.**

---

# 4. BUILDING RAPPORT IN FIRST MEETINGS

## 4.1. Safe Phrases

- **It's great to meet you.**
- **I'm glad to work with you on this project.**
- **We're looking forward to working closely with your team.**

## 4.2. Introducing Yourself

- **I'm [name], the Team Lead from [team/company].**
- **I'll be supporting this project from our side.**

## 4.3. Creating a Cooperative Atmosphere

- **We're excited to get started.**
- **We hope to build a smooth collaboration with your team.**

---

# 5. LIGHT CHECK-IN QUESTIONS

## 5.1. Safe Questions

- **How has your week been so far?**
- **How are things on your side?**
- **Has it been a busy week for your team?**

## 5.2. Responding to Short Answers

- **Glad to hear that.**
- **That's good to hear.**
- **I'm sorry to hear that.**

## 5.3. Keeping It Brief

- **Hope things are going well on your side.**

---

# 6. TRANSITIONING FROM SMALL TALK TO THE AGENDA

## 6.1. Safe Transition Phrases

- **With that, let's get started.**
- **Shall we move on to today's update?**
- **Let's jump into the main agenda.**
- **If that sounds good, I'll start with the project update.**

## 6.2. More Natural Alternatives

- **Alright, let's get into it.**
- **So, let me walk you through the latest update.**

---

# 7. KEEPING A FRIENDLY ATMOSPHERE DURING THE MEETING

## 7.1. Agreeing

- **That makes sense.**
- **That sounds good.**
- **Thanks, that's helpful.**

## 7.2. Acknowledging Input

- **That's a good point.**
- **Thanks for bringing that up.**
- **That's helpful feedback.**

## 7.3. Keeping a Soft Tone

- **I see.**
- **Understood.**
- **That's fair.**

---

# 8. POSITIVE RESPONSES AND THANKS

## 8.1. Thanking During a Meeting

- **Thanks for the clarification.**
- **Thanks for the update.**
- **Thanks for sharing that.**

## 8.2. Acknowledging Support

- **We really appreciate your support on this.**
- **That was very helpful for us.**

## 8.3. More Natural Alternatives

- **Thanks, that really helps.**
- **We appreciate the quick feedback.**

---

# 9. ENDING THE MEETING NATURALLY

## 9.1. Safe and Professional

- **Thank you very much for your time today.**
- **Thanks everyone for joining.**
- **It was great speaking with you today.**

## 9.2. Warmer and More Natural

- **Thanks again, everyone. Have a great rest of your day.**
- **Thanks for the discussion today. Talk to you again next week.**
- **Take care, everyone. See you next time.**

## 9.3. Scheduling the Next Meeting

- **See you at the same time next week.**
- **We'll catch up again on [day] at [time].**
- **Looking forward to speaking with you again next week.**

---

# 10. PHRASES TO AVOID

## 10.1. Too Casual with People You Don't Know Well

### ❌ Avoid

- **Hey guys, what's up?**

### ✅ Safer

- **Hi everyone, I hope you're doing well.**

## 10.2. Too Personal

### ❌ Avoid

- **Why do you look tired today?**

### ✅ Safer

- **I hope you're doing okay today.**

## 10.3. Small Talk That Goes On Too Long

- Avoid talking too much before getting into the main content

# CONCLUSION

Good small talk is not about saying a lot.

You just need to:

- open friendly
- keep the atmosphere comfortable
- transition smoothly into the agenda
- close politely

> **Final tip:** If you don't know what to say, one warm greeting and one transition sentence is all you need.
